Spectral centroid (brightness)

Roughly the “center of mass” of the spectrum. Higher values mean more energy in the high frequencies, so the sound is perceived as brighter or more “treble-heavy”. We map this to the Dark / Neutral / Bright tag.

Spectral bandwidth (complexity)

Measures how wide the spectrum is around the centroid. A wide bandwidth means the sound spreads across many frequencies, which feels more complex. Narrow bandwidth feels more simple or tone-like. This drives the Simple / Complex tag.

RMS energy (loudness)

RMS (root-mean-square) energy is a measure of average power over time. Higher RMS values correspond to higher perceived loudness, which we show as the Quiet / Loud tag and the blue bar in each card.

Tags on each card

  • Brightness tag: based on spectral centroid thresholds (Dark, Neutral, Bright).
  • Complexity tag: based on spectral bandwidth (Simple or Complex).
  • Loudness tag: based on RMS energy (Quiet or Loud).

Together these give a quick “vibe summary” of each track at a glance.
